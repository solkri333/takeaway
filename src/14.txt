        <h2>Best Hentai From 1990s (Classics):</h2>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>1. La Blue Girl</h3>
            <p>One of the most iconic hentai series of the ’90s, known for its ninja-themed plot and supernatural elements. Besides being created by Toshio Maeda, this series is known for making fun of the tentacle genre of hentai and putting its own spin on things. Think of it like a “Gintama” for hentai if you will.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>2. Cool Devices</h3>
            <p>Cool Devices is a series of anthologies featuring a mix of controversial and experimental stories. It has that typical old-school look of the 1980s-1990s vibe that many anime (not just hentai) showcased very well. Plot: The series follows Miko Mido, a young ninja-in-training and the next leader of the Miroku ninja clan.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>3. Urotsukidoji: Legend of the Overfiend (1989-1990s onwards)</h3>
            <p>This is another series that was written and illustrated by Toshio Maeda. The manga came before the hentai series. Famous for its dark fantasy and horror themes, this series is a landmark in the erotic anime genre. There are newer pieces of content going as far as 2016 and beyond.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>4. Bible Black</h3>
            <p>This hentai has become one of the pinnacles of hentai content. It’s so famous that it’s one of the first to be mentioned in these conversations or conversations surrounding “hentai” in general. Though Bible Black became more prominent in the early 2000s, its initial release started in the late ’90s.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>5. Mezzo Forte (1998)</h3>
            <p>Mezzo Forte is an original hentai series, meaning it wasn’t adapted from any other material. It’s about mercenaries at its core. It became known for its mix of action and adult scenes, and it gained a cult following for its unique approach to storytelling.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>6. Shin Angel (1994)</h3>
            <p>The main character, Kousuke, has a crush on the high school student Shizuka who is his childhood friend. She joins the class as part of the “plot”. Based on the popular visual novel, it explores romance and drama alongside explicit content.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>7. Endless Serenade (1997)</h3>
            <p>A romantic and sensual story featuring detailed animation and an emotional narrative. Endless Serenade is a very serious, dark, and tense hentai with controversial aspects that may be off-putting for some people. For comparison, it’s comparable to “La Blue Girl” but the latter is lighter on the content.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>8. Twin Angels (1995)</h3>
            <p>Unlike other hentai, this is more toned down and doesn’t have some of the more controversial themes. It’s a mix of mystery and adult themes, featuring twin sisters solving a supernatural mystery. The story follows Yuji Mikage, a college student who manages his late brother’s business with the help of his brother’s fiancée, Satsuki Mine.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>9. Angel of Darkness (1994)</h3>
            <p>A horror-tinged series with an eerie and dark atmosphere, it became one of the standout series in its niche. Tentacle-style hentai began during this era, and Angel of Darkness was one of the many that kick-started the clichés and famous trends decades later. The series takes place at Siren Academy, a prestigious boarding school for girls. Professor Goda, a young science professor, breaks a seal on a tree and is attacked and possessed by a tentacle monster.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>10. Dragon Knight (1991)</h3>
            <p>A fantasy-themed hentai series inspired by its video game counterpart, blending adventure and eroticism. Dragon Knight writes like a modern Isekai plot, but of course, this is where all of these ideas started. Plot points: Dragon Knight is about a swordsman named Takeru who enters a deserted town and is caught stealing food. A young woman named Luna believes Takeru is the man who is destined to save the area.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>11. Words Worth (1993)</h3>
            <p>Words Worth is a high-fantasy hentai with an engaging storyline about warring factions and a mysterious tablet. Its mix of adventure and adult content made it a classic of the era. Fun fact: The English dub features real-life porn actresses Jenna Jameson and Nikki Dial. It was adapted from a video game.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>12. Immoral Sisters (1999)</h3>
            <p>A family-centered drama with mature themes, known for its emotional conflicts. Blackmail is at the center of the plot after Yukie, one of the characters, has a traffic accident and has to fork out money.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>13. Night Shift Nurses (1998)</h3>
            <p>Night Shift Nurses gained notoriety for its controversial themes, but it remains one of the most recognized titles of its time. The story begins with a man named Sora lying in front of a hospital, seriously injured and close to death. As the famous meme says, “We already know where this is going.”</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>14. Cream Lemon (1984-ongoing)</h3>
            <p>Cream Lemon, a complex hentai as far as the various types of content it offers up. This long-running series continued through the ’90s with new installments, exploring diverse themes of romance, comedy, and drama with explicit content. Female characters are enjoyable thanks to their personality types or are used to fulfill “plot” points.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>15. Kite (1998)</h3>
            <p>Kite is a revenge plot-type hentai series that starts out with an understandable purpose. Sawa, the main character, is orphaned after her parents are murdered. She then targets specific people as a result (after being trained by an assassin who took her in). While not traditional hentai, this dark action anime included explicit scenes and had an adult-oriented narrative, making it relevant to the genre.</p>
        </div>

        <div class="list-item">
            <h3>16. Dark Love (1999)</h3>
            <p>A title that pushed the boundaries of the genre with its darker themes, leaving a lasting impression on viewers who preferred intense narratives. Quick summary of the plot: Gojou Tetsuya visits his distant aunt Koujin Rikka to borrow money for his childhood friend Utsumi Ayaka’s operation. Rikka orders Tetsuya to take beautiful women into the house to train them as prostitutes in return for lending money.</p>
        </div>
